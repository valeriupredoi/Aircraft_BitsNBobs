# Douglas C-47B *Lilly Belle II*

### USAAF Serial **42-100766** — History Summary

## Overview

**Aircraft type:** Douglas C-47B
**Construction number:** 26501
**Squadron:** 89th Troop Carrier Squadron
**Group:** 438th Troop Carrier Group, Ninth Air Force
**Base:** RAF Greenham Common, Newbury, Berkshire (UK)
**WWII Markings:** **4U-D**, nicknamed **“Lilly Bell II”**

## Timeline

### ✔ Construction & Assignment

* Built as a Douglas C-47B, c/n **26501**.
* Assigned to the **89th Troop Carrier Squadron**, part of the **438th Troop Carrier Group**, operating under the U.S. Ninth Air Force in the European Theater.

### ✔ WWII Operations (1944)

* Operated from **Greenham Common**, Berkshire, UK.
* Used for troop transport, supply runs, and support of airborne operations involving the 438th TCG.
* Carried squadron codes **4U-D** and the name **“Lilly Bell II.”**

### ✔ Accident & Loss — 25 October 1944

* Crashed during a routine flight from Greenham Common to France.
* Cause: A load shift likely caused the center of gravity to move aft, leading to a stall and inverted dive.
* **Four USAAF airmen were killed.**
* A memorial plaque (UK) marks the site and commemorates the crew.
* Article on the crash and current research on it <https://guildford-dragon.com/the-wwii-crash-of-c47-lilly-bell-ii/>

### ✔ Postwar / Preservation

* An alternate airframe has been displayed in the UK, including at the **Wings Museum** (Balcombe) <https://www.airhistory.net/photo/572005/42-100766/2100766>
* Often photographed and listed in aviation museum archives under serial **42-100766** and/or by its wartime name.
