Romania was one of the earlier Warsaw Pact operators of the **Mil Mi-8 "Hip"** family, introducing the type in **1968**. The Mi-8 served alongside the older Mi-4 before gradually giving way to domestically-built French-designed helicopters such as the IAR 316 Alouette III and IAR 330 Puma. ([Wikipedia][1])

Romania acquired approximately:

* **25 Mi-8T** transport helicopters
* **14 Mi-8PS** VIP/passenger helicopters

The Mi-8T was the standard military transport version, while the Mi-8PS ("Passenger Salon") featured a more comfortable interior and was used primarily for transporting senior government and military officials. ([Wikipedia][2])

### Roles in Romanian service

The Mi-8 was one of Romania's most versatile helicopters, performing missions including:

* Tactical troop transport
* Cargo transport (internal and underslung loads)
* Medical evacuation
* Search and rescue
* VIP transport
* Disaster relief
* Engineering support, such as lifting construction materials into mountainous areas

Unlike many Warsaw Pact countries, Romania also made extensive civilian use of the Mi-8 for infrastructure projects, including power-line construction in difficult terrain. ([roaf.ro][3])

### Air Force service

During the Cold War, Mi-8s equipped several helicopter units and operated alongside:

* Mi-4
* IAR 316 (licensed Alouette III)
* IAR 330 Puma

As Romania expanded domestic helicopter production during the 1970s and 1980s, the Romanian-built IAR 330 gradually assumed many transport duties. Nevertheless, Mi-8s remained valuable for heavier lifting and specialist missions. ([roaf.ro][4])

### After the Cold War

Following the fall of communism:

* Most Air Force Mi-8s were retired during the 1990s.
* Several VIP-configured aircraft remained in government service.
* Some Mi-8s and newer Mi-17s continued operating with Romania's Ministry of Internal Affairs (MAI), particularly for police aviation, emergency response, and civil protection. ([Wikipedia][2])

One notable modernization program occurred around 2012–2013, when at least one VIP Mi-8 underwent a major overhaul and avionics upgrade in St. Petersburg, extending its service life. ([Romania Military][5])

### Final retirement

Russia's invasion of Ukraine in 2022 had a significant impact on Romania's remaining Russian-built helicopter fleet. Sanctions and the difficulty of obtaining spare parts and overhaul support accelerated retirement plans.

The Ministry of Internal Affairs retired its remaining **Mi-8 and Mi-17 helicopters in 2022**, replacing them with Sikorsky S-70M Black Hawks for emergency, firefighting, and rescue missions. ([Wikipedia][6])

### Interesting facts

* Romania introduced the Mi-8 in the same period that it was beginning licensed helicopter production of Western designs—a unique situation within the Warsaw Pact.
* The Mi-8 often worked alongside Romanian-built IAR helicopters rather than replacing them outright.
* Romanian Mi-8s were used extensively in non-military engineering and utility work, reflecting Romania's emphasis on using military aviation assets for national infrastructure projects during the communist era. ([Wikipedia][1])

Overall, although the Mi-8 never became as iconic in Romania as the domestically built IAR 330 Puma, it provided more than five decades of reliable service in military, governmental, and civil-support roles before its final retirement.
