## 58 years apart: recovery of Apollo 8 and Artemis II

NASA's Orion spacecraft carried Artemis II Commander Reid Wiseman, Pilot Victor Glover, and Mission Specialist Christina Koch from NASA, along with Mission Specialist Jeremy Hansen from the CSA (Canadian Space Agency), and splashed down in the Pacific Ocean near San Diego, California, at 5:07 p.m. PDT, (8:07 p.m. EDT) on Friday, April 10, 2026. The Artemis II test flight launched on Wednesday, April 1, from NASA’s Kennedy Space Center in Florida to begin its 10-day journey around the Moon. NASA’s Landing and Recovery team and the U.S. military coordinated efforts to extract the Artemis II crew from the Orion spacecraft. USS John P. Murtha (LPD-26), a San Antonio-class amphibious transport dock ship, was tasked with the recovery of Artemis II astronauts, and the Orion module. For that purpose, HSC-23 (Helicopter Sea Combat 23) based NAS North Island, San Diego lifted at least two of their helicopetrs. They operate the MH-60S Seahawk (Knighthawk) helicopter, which is used for combat support, search and rescue, anti-surface warfare, and vertical replenishment. The two helicopters involved in the recovery were:

- MH-60S modex 42 BuNo 165754 or 167857
- MH-60S modex 44 BuNo 166290

On the other hand, 58 years ago, the helicopter that recovered the Apollo 8 crew on December 27, 1968, was a Sikorsky SH-3D Sea King, famously known as "Helicopter 66" (or "Old 66"), from Anti-Submarine Squadron HS-4 “Black Knights”, BuNo 152711. Piloted by Commander Donald S. Jones from the USS Yorktown, this iconic helicopter was renowned for recovering multiple Apollo missions (11, 12, and 13).

I have yet to see decals for HSC-23 birds, but there are a few decal options for “Old 66” (at least in 1/48 scale); as far as I know, the only correct kit in a larger scale, of an MH-60S, is Academy’s 1/35 scale MH-60S 12120 (based on their old but good 1/35 Black Hawk kit).
